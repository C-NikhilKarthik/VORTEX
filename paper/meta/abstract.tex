\begin{abstract}
Real-time 6-DoF pose estimation on mobile and embedded platforms is constrained by limited computational resources and the need for geometric robustness. While dense SLAM systems and learned pose regression models provide high accuracy, they are often impractical for edge deployment. Classical feature-based tracking offers a lightweight alternative but relies on algebraic formulations, such as homography estimation, that lack explicit 3D constraints and degrade under viewpoint changes, scale variation, and partial visibility.

We present \textbf{\textit{VORTEX}} (Visual Object Rendering and Tracking Engine for Cross-Platform), a hardware- and geometry-aware tracking framework designed for edge devices. The system combines a SIMD-optimized CPU pipeline with a GPU-accelerated feature pipeline for efficient keypoint detection, descriptor extraction, and matching. Building on this, we introduce a render-and-track formulation on the $\mathrm{SE}(3)$ manifold, enabling direct 2D--3D correspondence lifting through synthetic reference rendering.

To improve stability, we propose a physics-inspired pose refinement method in which correspondences are modeled as virtual springs. Residual magnitudes define adaptive weights, yielding a weighted Gauss--Newton update that suppresses outliers and improves convergence under partial visibility and non-uniform feature distributions.

Experiments on Android devices demonstrate that while GPU acceleration improves throughput, the proposed geometry-aware refinement significantly enhances pose stability and robustness for real-time 6-DoF tracking without persistent mapping.
\end{abstract}
